Terhathum, Eastern Nepal · 27.13°N 87.55°E

Echoes of Eastern Nepal

A hilltown where the clouds settle in for breakfast and leave by noon. This is a visual journal of the place I call home — its lakes, its ridgelines, and the quiet way it holds onto both tradition and progress.

Stop 01 · The Lake
01 / Stillness

A hidden mirror in the hills

Right near home, this pond turns still every morning before the wind picks up — long enough to hold a perfect double of the sky. The mist rolls in low across the water first, then burns off into the kind of cloudscape that makes the walk to school feel like a detour through a postcard.

02 / Reverence

Where the wind carries prayers

Gufa Pokhari sits quietly above the town, a single wind-shaped tree on an island of rock, wrapped in prayer flags that catch every breeze. People come here for peace, not spectacle — and the lake gives it back, one still reflection at a time.

Stop 03 · The Road
03 / Progress

Nature and progress, hand in hand

Terhathum's roads have quietly transformed how people move through these hills — smoother blacktop cutting through terrain that used to take twice as long to cross. What hasn't changed is the view: mountains on one side, sky on the other, the whole way.

The person behind the lens

Written & photographed by Chalsh Guragain

I grew up in Terhathum before moving to Kathmandu to work in networking and security. This journal is a small way of keeping the hills close — the lakes I walked past on the way to school, the ridgeline that never looks the same way twice, and the roads that now make it easier for everyone to come see it for themselves.

— Chalsh, Terhathum → Kathmandu
